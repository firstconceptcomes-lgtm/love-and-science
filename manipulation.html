Manipulation & Safety

Identify common manipulation tactics in dating and relationships, and discover practical safety measures to protect your emotional well-being.

Recognize the Signs

Why Manipulation Awareness Matters

Manipulation in relationships can be subtle and damaging, eroding trust and self-esteem over time. Learning to recognize these tactics empowers you to set boundaries, seek help when needed, and build healthier, more respectful connections.

Common Manipulation Tactics

Gaslighting

A tactic where someone makes you doubt your own reality, memory, or perceptions, often leading to confusion and dependency on the manipulator.

Love Bombing

Overwhelming affection and attention early in a relationship to gain control, followed by withdrawal to create emotional dependency.

Guilt Tripping

Using guilt or obligation to influence behavior, making you feel responsible for the manipulator's emotions or problems.

Silent Treatment

Withholding communication as punishment, creating anxiety and forcing concessions to restore connection.

Triangulation

Involving a third party to create jealousy, competition, or doubt, often to deflect attention or gain leverage.
