Core Mechanisms of Love

Psychology of Attraction

Initial attraction isn't random. It’s shaped by familiarity (we like what we know), similarity (shared values & interests), proximity, and subconscious cues. The brain’s reward system floods with dopamine, creating excitement, motivation, and that addictive "can't stop thinking about them" feeling.

Emotional Bonding & Attachment

Long-term love relies on oxytocin (the "cuddle hormone") and vasopressin. These chemicals build trust, reduce stress, and deepen emotional safety. Consistent care, vulnerability, and mutual support strengthen these neural pathways over time.

Heart-Brain Connection

Love isn't just emotion — it's biology. The heart and brain are in constant dialogue. Romantic love activates the same reward circuits as food, money, and drugs, while secure attachment calms the amygdala (fear center) and boosts prefrontal cortex activity (rational thinking & empathy).
