The Four Attachment Styles

Secure Attachment

Comfortable with both intimacy and independence. Secure individuals trust their partners, communicate openly, and handle conflict constructively. They feel worthy of love and expect reliability from others.

Anxious Attachment

Strong desire for closeness paired with fear of abandonment. People with anxious attachment often seek constant reassurance, worry about their partner's feelings, and can become overly sensitive to signs of rejection.

Avoidant Attachment

High value on independence and self-reliance. Avoidant individuals may downplay emotions, keep partners at a distance, and feel uncomfortable with too much closeness or vulnerability.

Fearful-Avoidant (Disorganized)

Deep desire for connection mixed with intense fear of getting hurt. This style often leads to push-pull dynamics — craving intimacy one moment and withdrawing the next due to past trauma or inconsistency.

Ready to Understand Yourself Better?

Attachment styles aren't fixed — with awareness and effort, anyone can move toward more secure patterns.
